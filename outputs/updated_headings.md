# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)


## Why do hospitals open allied health travel jobs?
  - To fill staffing gaps during high census periods, staff leaves, or seasonal busy times. Some facilities regularly use travelers for predictable busy seasons.  

## Does Aya Healthcare have a mobile app?
  - Yes. Download from Google Play or the Apple App Store to search travel, per‑diem, and permanent positions.  

## Do I need a license to work as a travel allied health professional?
  - Licensing requirements vary by profession and state. Aya’s licensing department can assist and may cover the cost. Examples:  
    - Radiology: National certification + state license (most states).  
    - RRTs: National registration + state license.  
    - Lab: National certification; many states require a state license (CA, NY, FL, HI, LA, MT, NV, ND, TN, WV).  
    - CST: National certification preferred; some states require a state license (ID, IN, MA, NV, NJ, NY, SC, OR, TN; TX only state license).  

## How do I become a travel allied health professional?
  1. Create an Aya account – register and upload your resume.  
  2. Aya matches you with open assignments and assigns a recruiter.  
  3. Your recruiter works with you to set goals and secure your first assignment, then guides your travel career.  

## What allied health travel jobs does Aya staff?
  - Ultrasound technologists, radiologic technologists, physical therapists, occupational therapists, respiratory therapists, speech‑language pathologists, and many more. Search open jobs at https://www.ayahealthcare.com/allied‑travel‑careers/.  

## Does Aya pay for licenses?
  - For many professions, Aya will pay or reimburse licensing fees and can help expedite the process.  

## How long are travel assignments?
  - Standard length: 13 weeks. Shorter or longer contracts are also available. You can also take breaks between assignments.  

## When should I apply?
  - Apply whenever you’re ready. Some travelers wait months or a year before accepting; others start almost immediately. Having a complete profile speeds up the process.  

## How much experience is required?
  - Minimum one year of recent experience in your field; some facilities may require more. Your recruiter can confirm specific requirements.  

## Can I travel with family, a partner, or friends?
  - Yes. Aya can help coordinate assignments that align with travel partners.  

## Can I travel with a pet?
  - Absolutely. Discuss pet‑friendly housing options with your recruiter.  

## How do I find housing?
  - Aya may offer housing directly, or you can receive a housing stipend to find your own accommodation. Discuss options with your recruiter.  

## How am I paid?
  - Weekly via direct deposit. A payroll specialist verifies hours each week and is your point of contact for any pay questions.  

## Do travel jobs include benefits?
  - Yes. From day 1 you receive medical, dental, vision, sick‑pay accrual, 401(k) eligibility after four consecutive months, and exclusive discounts.  

## What support does Aya provide?
  - A dedicated team: recruiter, credentialing specialist, payroll representative, experience specialist, and a clinical support team for on‑assignment issues.  

## How much orientation will I receive?
  - Orientation length varies by facility; Aya ensures you get enough training to start confidently.  

## What is the “Your Team” support model?
  - Recruiter (career guidance), Payroll Specialist (accurate, on‑time pay), Credentialing Specialist (document tracking & compliance), Experience Specialist (overall satisfaction, onboarding, benefits, housing).  

## What is the 24‑hour emergency contact?
  - A dedicated emergency line is available around the clock for any urgent issues that arise while on assignment.

## RN Degree
Minimum Associate of Science in Nursing (ASN) or higher.

## NCLEX‑RN
Must have passed the National Council Licensure Examination.

## State License
Valid RN license in the state of practice; Aya assists with obtaining or converting a license for compact states.

## Work Experience
Minimum 1 year in specialty (hospital setting preferred); some facilities require 2+ years or prior travel experience.

## Medical & Health Requirements
Upload health records; arrange blood work, vaccinations, TB testing, and physical exam as needed. Aya’s credentialing specialist can schedule missing services.

## Drug Screen & Background Check
Completed prior to assignment; Aya schedules appointments and tracks completion.

Facility‑specific requirements are reviewed; Aya helps with renewals, additional certifications, and provides cost assistance (some fees covered up‑front, others reimbursable).

# Regulatory & Licensing Details


## RN Licensure
Must hold a valid state RN license; Aya assists with state‑specific licensing, compact state conversion, and fee reimbursement.

## NCLEX‑RN
Required for all registered nurses; Aya verifies passing status.

## Advanced Practice Licensure
For CRNAs, NPs, and APPs; Aya supports credentialing and state requirements.

## Allied Health Licensure
Varies by profession; Aya helps obtain or renew radiology, respiratory therapy, lab, CST, and other certifications.

Mandatory pre‑employment; scheduled by Aya’s credentialing specialist.

## Vaccinations & Health Exams
Facility‑specific requirements (TB test, flu shot, COVID‑19 vaccination, physical exam). Aya coordinates services when needed.
